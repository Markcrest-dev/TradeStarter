Risk Management Guide

The most important skill in trading is not making money, but keeping it.

1. The 1% Rule

Never risk more than 1-2% of your total trading capital on a single trade. This ensures that even a losing streak won't wipe out your account.

Example: If you have a $10,000 account, your maximum loss per trade should be $100 (1%).

2. Stop Losses

A Stop Loss is non-negotiable. It is your insurance policy against catastrophic loss. Place your stop loss at a technical invalidation point, not just a random dollar amount.

3. Risk-to-Reward Ratio (R:R)

Only take trades where the potential profit is significantly higher than the potential risk. Aim for a minimum of 1:2 R:R.

Why? With a 1:2 R:R, you can be wrong 50% of the time and still be profitable.

4. Position Sizing

Position sizing is how you determine how many units to buy or sell. It should be based on your risk amount, not how confident you feel.

5. Psychology & Discipline

Risk management is 90% psychology. Stick to your plan. Do not move your stop loss further away to "give the trade room." Do not revenge trade after a loss.
